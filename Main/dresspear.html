How to dress for

Pear

Tips
  • Highlight the upper body

    • - adding more weight to the shoulder and bust area

    - Wear bright colours on your upper body

    - Layering on your top half creates visual interest and draws the eye upwards

    - Wear fitted clothes to accentuate the waist

  • Play down the lower body

    • - Wear darker colours on your lower body

    - Avoid skinny and tight bottoms that emphasise the wide hips

    - Avoid details, patterns, pockets on your thigh and hip area and belts on your hips

    Tops

    Bright colours, bold patterns, details and embellishments around the bust and shoulders. Tops should not go beyond the hip

    puff sleeve
    flutter-sleeve
    ruffle tops
    stripes tops
    Skirts

    Skirts with a wider shape at the bottom are effective in balancing out the hips.Skirt length is knee-length or between the knee and mid-calf. This length helps to stretch the body visually.

    tulip skirts
    A-line skirts
    bias skirts
    panelled skirts
    Trousers

    Trousers should be mid- or high-waisted to hug in the defined waist or darker colours, clean lines and simple stitching to avoid drawing attention to the lower body.

    straight trousers
    flare trousers
    wide-leg trousers
    bootcut trousers
    Dress

    Dresses with a wider shape at the bottom are more effective in balancing out the hips.Great dress choices are A-line or X-line dresses.

    wrap dress
    empire dress
    tulip dress
